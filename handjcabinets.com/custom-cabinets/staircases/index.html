Structure & Design
The Product

What goes into a
custom staircase?

A custom staircase is the structural and visual centerpiece of many luxury homes. It connects levels, anchors the entry, and communicates the standard of the entire interior in a way that no other single element can. Designed correctly, a staircase elevates the room around it. Designed poorly, it undermines everything.

Custom staircase work encompasses the structural components — carriages, stringers, treads, risers — as well as the visual elements: newel posts, balusters, railings, and the finish of every surface that the eye touches. At H & J, we approach staircase projects as architectural commissions, coordinating with the structural engineer, the architect, and the interior designer throughout.

Engineering & Detail

What separates a great
staircase from an ordinary one?

Structurally, a great staircase is engineered for the specific span, load, and material. Every tread must be the same depth, every riser the same height — because the human body learns the pattern within a few steps and a deviation is felt immediately, even when it is barely measurable. Getting those tolerances right requires precision that most stair contractors do not hold.

Visually, a great staircase is defined by its proportions, its material quality, and the consistency of its detail work. A newel post that is undersized for the stair, a baluster spacing that reads as crowded, or a railing profile that does not coordinate with the trim throughout the home — any of these undermines the installation regardless of the material cost.

  • Structural precisionTreads and risers built to consistent tolerance — every step identical so the stair feels right from the first step.
  • Premium solid wood treadsWhite oak, walnut, and maple treads selected for grain consistency and finished to furniture grade.
  • Custom railing and balustradeNewel posts, balusters, and railings designed for the specific stair and the architectural language of the home.
  • Coordinated finish throughoutPaint, stain, and clear coat finishes matched to the surrounding trim and cabinetry.

Staircases that anchor
the home

H & J builds staircases as part of a complete interior millwork program. The stair profile, the balustrade design, and the finish system are all designed in relationship to the doors, cabinetry, and trim throughout the home. When the staircase reads as part of the same interior language as everything else, the result is a home that feels composed and intentional at every level.

We have built staircases that are the primary visual element of an entry foyer, staircases that coordinate with paneled walls and coffered ceilings, and staircases that carry a simple, clean profile designed to disappear and let the surrounding architecture lead. Every approach begins with the right structural foundation and is finished to a fine furniture standard.
