First Impressions
The Product

What are
custom entry doors?

A custom entry door is the architectural threshold of a home — the first element a visitor experiences and the one that communicates the standard of everything that follows. Built to exact opening dimensions and finished to match or complement the exterior architecture, a custom door is the opposite of a catalogue selection dropped into a rough opening.

Custom entry doors can be built in nearly any architectural language: traditional paneled with carved detail, contemporary with glass lights and minimal profile, transitional with clean lines and warm wood, or bold and monolithic in solid hardwood. The starting point is always the architecture of the home and the statement the client wants to make.

Construction & Security

What makes a premium
entry door?

An entry door bears structural, security, and weatherproofing demands that interior doors do not. The stiles and rails must be sized and joined to resist racking under load. The core must be dense enough to contribute to both security and acoustic separation. The finish must be formulated for exterior exposure — UV resistance, moisture tolerance, and long-term stability in coastal climates.

Hardware selection is equally critical. Entry hardware — locksets, deadbolts, hinges, and threshold systems — must be specified at a grade that matches the door it serves. Undersized or poorly installed hardware is the most common failure point in otherwise well-built entry door installations.

  • Exterior-grade finish systemsMulti-coat UV-resistant finishes formulated for coastal climates and long-term performance.
  • Premium entry hardwareLocksets, deadbolts, and hinges specified at architectural grade — installed and adjusted for precise operation.
  • Weather and acoustic sealingFull-perimeter compression seals, adjustable threshold systems, and acoustic-rated cores where required.
